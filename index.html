Cell interconnect strips

Dimension the nickel strips that join the cells. The drawing shows the repeating unit of the pack — two parallel groups (A, B) connected in series through P−1 bridges. Currents assume balanced cells (each sources I ÷ P) and resistivity at 20 °C; the limits are conservative rules of thumb for welded strip with limited cooling, not physical maxima. Moving particles show the direction of the current in each strip — more and redder particles mean that part runs closer to its recommended limit. The faint strip below the B cells is the output collector strip on their far terminal (relevant when B is the pack's last group): each B cell delivers its I ÷ P share into it. Click the round points to set/unset cable taps there — the strip currents recalculate around your taps (tap leads assumed ideal and joined). It uses the group-bridge width and does not affect the interconnect above.
